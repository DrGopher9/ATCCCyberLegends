# Week 17 — Lab Exercises: The Inject Workflow

Injects are a **team** workstream with an owner and a clock. You'll receive a set with staggered
deadlines, execute them, and submit **PDF** responses to White-Team standard. Templates:
[`inject-templates.md`](../../CCDC-main-Matt_2026/CCDC-main/competition-tools/inject-templates.md) and
[`CCDC_2026/Injects/`](../../CCDC_2026/Injects/).

---

## The workflow (run it for every inject)
1. **Receive** — injects owner logs it: number, title, deadline, point value, parts.
2. **Assign** — owner + captain assign it to whoever's best placed (often the relevant specialist);
   confirm ownership out loud.
3. **Execute** — do the work (technical) or draft the response (business).
4. **Write** — use the template; address **every part**; professional tone; specific.
5. **Verify** — technical injects: confirm it works and **no scored service broke**.
6. **Submit** — as a **PDF**, to the right place, **before the deadline**.
7. **Log** — mark it done on the board; note submission time.

## Drill 1 — A technical inject
Example: *"Create a new user `jsmith` on the mail system with access to email, and provide the account
details and the security measures applied. Due in 25 minutes."*
- [ ] Owner assigned; deadline on the board
- [ ] Create the account correctly (don't break POP3/mail auth — coordinate with AD)
- [ ] Verify it works
- [ ] Write the response from the "New User Account" template (details + security measures + verification)
- [ ] Submit as PDF before the deadline; log it

## Drill 2 — A business inject
Example: *"The CEO wants a one-page memo describing your password policy and why it protects the company.
Due in 30 minutes."*
- [ ] Owner assigned (often the captain/injects owner, freeing specialists for boxes)
- [ ] Write a professional, specific one-page memo — addressed to the CEO, no slang, no team-identifying
      info beyond what's asked
- [ ] Submit as PDF; log it

## Drill 3 — A multi-part inject
Example: *"(a) Produce a network diagram of your environment. (b) List the services you are protecting
and their business purpose. (c) Identify your two highest risks and your mitigation. Due in 40 minutes."*
- [ ] Split the parts across owners if needed, but **one person integrates** the final PDF
- [ ] Address **every** part (partial answers lose the parts you skip)
- [ ] Submit one clean PDF; log it

## Drill 4 — Injects under fire
The facilitator introduces a light fire (a service hiccup) mid-set. Practice: the injects owner keeps
the deadline clock; the captain decides whether to pull the specialist to the fire or keep them on the
inject; **deadlines are not missed** because of a distraction.

## Grade yourselves (White-Team rubric)
For each submission, score 1–5 on: **correct format**, **addresses every part**, **specificity**,
**professional tone**, **on time / correct submission**. Compare to a coach's grade.

## Done?
You've hit the objectives if the team completed a mixed set (technical + business + multi-part) as PDFs,
on time, to White-Team standard, with an owner running the clock. Practice writing one inject response
solo for [`homework.md`](homework.md).
