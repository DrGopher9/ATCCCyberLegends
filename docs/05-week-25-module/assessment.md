## The decision
- **Green light →** the team is **competition-ready.** Move to Phase 5 (taper): keep skills warm, lock
  logistics, rest, compete. Do **not** add new content or heavy scrimmages — protect the peak.
- **Not yet →** name the specific residual risk. Options: one more focused scrimmage early in Phase 5,
  or a concrete plan to manage the risk at the qualifier (e.g. a known-weak box gets the strongest
  operator + a dedicated backup). Be honest — a named, managed risk is fine; an ignored one is not.

## Phase 4 exit
Met when the green-light checklist is confirmed (or residual risk named + owned) and the team transitions
to the taper. From here, Phase 5 keeps the team sharp and rested through to the Minnesota qualifier.

> The arc is complete: a brand-new team in July is, by late January, a coordinated unit that holds
> services, completes injects, evicts a real red team, documents cleanly, and sustains a 7-hour day.
> That's what the green light means.
