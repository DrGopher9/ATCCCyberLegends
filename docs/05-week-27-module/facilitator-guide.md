# Week 27 — Facilitator Guide: Qualifier Week (Brief, Rest, Compete)

> **Phase 5, Week 27 — the qualifier.** Built from [`../templates/module-template.md`](../templates/module-template.md).

**Rubric targets:** execute everything the program built — hit the capstone dimensions live at the
**Minnesota CCDC qualifier** ([`../02-readiness-rubric.md`](../02-readiness-rubric.md),
[`../07-competition-reference.md`](../07-competition-reference.md)).

**Council lens:** all five, but the **Coach** and **Former Tech Lead** lead the week — the technical work
is done; now it's about a calm, rested, coordinated team executing. The job this week is to **not
overdo it**: a final light touch, a clear brief, rest, and a confident, professional performance.

**You need:** the locked roster/kit/logistics from Week 26, the confirmed competition details, and a
calm plan for the day.

---

## The week's shape
- **Early week:** one final **light** skill-maintenance touch (a crisp First-30 + a couple of reflexes).
  Nothing heavy. Confirm the kit and logistics one last time.
- **Mid/late week:** **rest.** No new content, no scrimmages. A rested team beats a fried one.
- **Day before:** short pre-competition brief; early night.
- **Competition day:** execute (below).

## Pre-competition brief (day before or morning-of)
- [ ] Roles + backups confirmed; captain runs the floor, doesn't keyboard
- [ ] The plan: open with the **First 30 Minutes**; priority order **protect services → evict → injects
      → harden**
- [ ] Comms protocol: incidents in format, one owner per incident, clean handoffs
- [ ] **"If X breaks, who owns it"** for each box + backup
- [ ] Injects: owner watches the portal; **responses are PDFs**; deadlines are hard; **times are CST**
- [ ] Rules review: keep ICMP up except PA core; nothing that breaks the scoring engine; no VM revert
      (cold boot only, scrubs are penalized/limited); professional conduct; **no contact with the red
      team during hours**
- [ ] Energy plan: breaks, food, backup rotations across the 7-hour day
- [ ] Documentation from minute one; file IR reports for real exploitation events (not padded)

## Competition day
Run the season's playbook. See [`lab-exercises.md`](lab-exercises.md) for the day-of runbook. Coaches/
advisers **may not assist during scoring** (packet rule) — the team is on its own, which is exactly what
the last seven months prepared them for.

## Immediately after — team debrief
- Run a short, positive [AAR](../templates/aar-template.md) while it's fresh: what went well, what was
  hard, what you'd do at **regionals** (if you advance). Capture specifics for the regional bridge.
- Celebrate the season regardless of result — a brand-new team that competed a full 7-hour qualifier as
  a coordinated unit has already succeeded.

## Facilitator notes & common snags
- **Don't peak too early or cram late.** The taper only works if you hold the line on rest this week.
- **Calm is contagious.** A calm captain and coach keep the team out of panic-driven self-owns. The
  biggest day-of risk is a rattled team breaking its own services.
- **Trust the training.** The team has run this day (Week 24). Remind them: they've done this before.
- **Professionalism scores.** Comms with the White Team, inject tone, conduct — all judged. Keep it
  sharp to the last hour.

## Definition of done
The team competed at the Minnesota qualifier as a prepared, rested, coordinated unit, executed the
season's playbook, and debriefed. Win or not, the training goal — a competition-ready team — is met. If
they win, proceed to the **regional bridge** ([`05-regional-bridge-module/`](../05-regional-bridge-module/)).
