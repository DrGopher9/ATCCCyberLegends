# Regional Bridge — Facilitator Guide: State → Midwest Regional

> **Phase 5, contingent module** (only if the team wins the Minnesota qualifier). Built from
> [`../templates/module-template.md`](../templates/module-template.md). Overview:
> [`../04-curriculum/phase-5-taper-qualifier.md`](../04-curriculum/phase-5-taper-qualifier.md).

**When:** the ~4–6 weeks between the MN qualifier (~late Jan) and the **Erich J. Spengler Midwest
Regional CCDC** (2026 was **March 20–21 at Purdue University Northwest**; confirm 2027). Winner of
regionals advances to NCCDC.

**Rubric targets:** hold the capstone bar under **harder** conditions — a tougher red team, more/faster
injects, and a larger service surface ([`../02-readiness-rubric.md`](../02-readiness-rubric.md)).

**Council lens:** the **Red Teamer** (regionals bring national-caliber attackers — assume they'll get
in; the bar is fast detection + eviction + reporting) and the **Coach** (a short, intense re-peak, driven
by the qualifier's exposed weaknesses, then a re-taper).

**You need:** the **qualifier AAR** (this drives everything), the full lab, the strongest available red
team, and — critically — awareness that **regionals may differ** (bigger environment, possibly onsite,
travel). Confirm the regional packet when available.

---

## The shape (compressed, AAR-driven)
This is a mini-Phase-4: re-peak, don't rebuild. The team is already competition-ready; the bridge
sharpens it for a harder field.

1. **Week A — Close the qualifier gaps + raise intensity.** Work the qualifier AAR's top issues. Run a
   full scrimmage with the **red team dialed above qualifier level.** AAR.
2. **Week B — Deepen + scale.** Deepen bench cross-training (regionals are longer / bigger — depth
   matters more), add any new services the regional environment introduces, drill faster injects. Full
   scrimmage. AAR.
3. **Week C (+ any extra) — Dress rehearsal + re-taper.** A full-length, regional-intensity dress
   rehearsal, then **re-taper** (light maintenance + rest + logistics for travel/onsite) into the event.

## Learning objectives
By the regional, the team can:
1. Hold the capstone bar against a **tougher** red team and a **larger** service load.
2. Handle a bigger/possibly-onsite environment and faster inject tempo.
3. Sustain performance with deeper bench rotation over a longer/harder event.

## Running it
- **Drive from the qualifier AAR.** The single best predictor of regional weaknesses is what the
  qualifier exposed. Fix those first.
- **Raise red-team intensity deliberately** above qualifier level, then re-taper — same principle as
  Phase 4, higher ceiling. Use the [scorecard](../templates/scrimmage-scorecard.md) + [AAR](../templates/aar-template.md).
- **Logistics shift:** regionals may be onsite/travel. Re-do the Week-26 logistics lock for the new
  venue (setup, connectivity, kit, roster, **confirm the repo/web-proxy rules for the regional**).
- **Protect the peak again:** re-taper the final days. Don't arrive at regionals fried.

## Facilitator notes & common snags
- **Don't rebuild — re-peak.** The team is ready; this is targeted sharpening, not a new season.
- **Regionals get harder late.** The national-caliber red team escalates; drill sustained, late-game
  vigilance and deeper rotation.
- **New services / bigger environment.** If the regional adds boxes/services, baseline + assign them
  fast (Phase-2 pattern, compressed). Don't let an unfamiliar box be an untended one.
- **Travel/onsite changes energy math.** Factor travel fatigue into the taper and the day-of plan.

## Definition of done
The team closed its qualifier gaps, held the capstone bar under above-qualifier intensity, scaled to the
regional environment, and re-tapered — arriving at the Midwest Regional sharp, deep, and rested.
