## The taper principle
The team peaked at the Week-24 dress rehearsal. From here, **maintain** — short, crisp skill-maintenance
so nothing goes cold, and **no exhausting scrimmages.** A rested team makes fewer self-inflicted
mistakes than a fried one, and self-owns are pure point loss.

## Learning objectives / outcomes for the week
1. **Roster locked:** the starting 8 (min 4, max 2 grad students) + alternates on the 12-person roster,
   decided on Phase 1–4 checklist + scrimmage performance.
2. **Logistics locked:** competition date/time (CST), remote-vs-onsite setup, accounts, connectivity,
   what's allowed in-room.
3. **GitHub URL submitted to the State Director** so the repo is reachable via the web proxy on game day
   (packet rule — miss it and you go in without your scripts).
4. **In-competition kit prepared** (printed materials are allowed; no USB).
5. Light skill-maintenance only; energy protection started.

## Weeknight session plan (2–3 hr)
- **0:00–0:20 — Warm-up (light):** a crisp team First-30 — maintenance, not a grind.
- **0:20–0:45 — Roster + roles:** finalize the starting 8, roles, and the backup for every box (Week-23
  grid). Communicate decisions clearly and kindly; alternates still have a real role.
- **0:45–1:15 — Logistics lock:** work the checklist in [`lab-exercises.md`](lab-exercises.md) —
  **submit the GitHub URL**, confirm accounts/connectivity, setup plan, timing (CST).
- **1:15–2:15 — Light maintenance drills:** one rep each of the highest-value reflexes (credential
  sweep, service recovery, an IR report, a comms call). Short and sharp.
- **2:15–2:30 — Assign [`homework.md`](homework.md) (kit + rest); preview Week 27 (final week).**

## Weekend lab plan (2–3 hr, light)
- **Warm-up (20m):** maintenance drill.
- **Kit build (60m):** assemble + print the in-competition kit (checklist below); each member preps
  their box's runbook for print.
- **Short confirmation scenario (45m):** a brief, low-stakes run to keep the machinery warm — **not** a
  full scrimmage. Confirm the green-light dimensions still hold; stop while the team feels good.
- **Logistics review + [`assessment.md`](assessment.md) (30m):** confirm the readiness/logistics
  checklist.

## Facilitator notes & common snags
- **Resist the urge to drill hard.** If someone wants "one more big scrimmage," explain the taper: the
  gains are banked; now you protect them. Light and sharp.
- **Roster decisions are emotional.** Be transparent about the criteria (performance, not seniority) and
  give alternates a clear, valued role — they may sub in, and they keep the starters honest.
- **The GitHub-URL submission is easy to forget and costly to miss.** Put it on the board and confirm it
  in writing.
- **Confirm against the real packet.** Dates/rules/services can shift year to year — reconcile the 2027
  packet into [`../07-competition-reference.md`](../07-competition-reference.md) if it's out.

## Definition of done
Roster + roles + logistics locked, GitHub URL submitted, kit assembled, green-light state maintained with
light drills, and energy protection underway. One week to go.
