# Week 21 — Facilitator Guide: Fix-It Week (Close the AAR Gaps)

> **Phase 4, Week 21.** Built from [`../templates/module-template.md`](../templates/module-template.md).
> Fix-it weeks are the other half of Phase 4's rhythm — a scrimmage without a fix-it week just rehearses
> mistakes.

**Rubric targets:** raise the weakest capstone dimensions from Week 20 toward the Week-24 targets
([`../02-readiness-rubric.md`](../02-readiness-rubric.md)); re-sharpen any slipped Tier-2/3 skills.

**Council lens:** the **Coach** owns fix-it weeks — turn the AAR's honest findings into concrete,
measured improvement. No new content and no scrimmage; this is deliberate practice on exactly what broke.

**You need:** Week 20's **scorecard and AAR top-3 fix list**, the lab for targeted drills, and the
per-member/rubric tracking sheet.

---

## The principle
Fix the **seams**, not the boxes. Week 20's failures are almost always team-level: an inject missed
under fire, an incident nobody owned, a stale board in hour 3, eviction that didn't stick, comms that
collapsed when three things broke at once. Drill those specifically.

## Learning objectives
By end of the week, the team can:
1. **Close each of Week 20's top-3 fixes** — verified, not just discussed.
2. Re-drill the weakest capstone dimension until it's demonstrably better.
3. Re-sharpen any individual Tier-2/3 skill that slipped under scrimmage pressure.

## Weeknight session plan (2–3 hr)
- **0:00–0:15 — Warm-up:** the weakest reflex from Week 20 (e.g. service recovery, IR report, a comms
  call).
- **0:15–0:30 — Own the AAR:** put the top-3 fixes on the board with owners + how each will be
  *verified* closed. Name the weakest scorecard dimension as the week's focus.
- **0:30–2:15 — Targeted drills:** run [`lab-exercises.md`](lab-exercises.md) — the specific drills for
  this team's gaps (built from the AAR). Coaches verify each fix closes.
- **2:15–2:30 — Assign [`homework.md`](homework.md); preview Week 22 (heavier scrimmage).**

## Weekend lab plan (3–4 hr)
- **Warm-up (20m):** re-drill a slipped Tier-2/3 skill per member.
- **Fix verification (90m):** each top-3 fix is **demonstrated closed** under realistic conditions (not
  "we talked about it" — show it working). Re-run the scenario that exposed each gap.
- **Weak-dimension focus (60m):** hammer the weakest capstone dimension — e.g. if injects were missed,
  run inject-under-fire reps; if eviction didn't stick, run detect→evict→re-check reps.
- **Assessment (30m):** run [`assessment.md`](assessment.md).
- **AAR-lite (10m):** are the top-3 actually closed? Anything to carry to Week 22?

## Facilitator notes & common snags
- **"Closed" means demonstrated.** A fix isn't closed because it was discussed — re-create the failing
  situation and show it now succeeds. Otherwise it re-appears in Week 22.
- **Don't drift into a mini-scrimmage.** Fix-it weeks are focused practice on named gaps, not another
  full run. Save intensity for Week 22.
- **Protect individual skills.** Scrimmages expose rusty fundamentals; use this week to re-drill them
  (a specialist who fumbled service recovery under stress needs reps, calmly).
- **Update the tracking sheet.** Note which dimensions/skills improved so Week 22 tests the right things.

## Definition of done
Every Week-20 top-3 fix is demonstrably closed, the weakest capstone dimension is measurably better, and
any slipped individual skills are re-sharpened. The team goes into Week 22 (heavier) with its Week-20
gaps actually fixed.
