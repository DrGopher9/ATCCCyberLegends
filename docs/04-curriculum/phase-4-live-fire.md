# Phase 4 — Live-Fire & AAR (Weeks 20–25, Dec–mid-Jan)

**Goal:** repetition against a real red team at increasing intensity, with a disciplined After-Action
Review after every scrimmage. This is where the team actually gets good.

**Council lens:** the Red Teamer brings the pressure; the Coach runs the AARs so the team *learns*
from each one instead of just surviving it. Alternate scrimmage weeks with fix-it weeks — cramming
scrimmages without fixing gaps just rehearses the same mistakes.

**Exit gate:** the team hits the **capstone scrimmage thresholds** in
[`02-readiness-rubric.md`](../02-readiness-rubric.md) during the Week 24 dress rehearsal.

## Weeks

| Wk | Focus |
|---|---|
| 20 | **Full scrimmage #1** — full service load, moderate red team — + AAR + prioritized fix list |
| 21 | **Fix-it week** — close the AAR gaps; re-drill the weakest Tier-2/3 skills |
| 22 | **Full scrimmage #2** — heavier red team, full inject load — + AAR |
| 23 | **Fix-it + cross-training** — every role gets a competent backup (bench depth) |
| 24 | **Dress rehearsal** — full-length scrimmage under qualifier conditions — + AAR, scored to capstone thresholds |
| 25 | **Final fixes** — close remaining gaps; confirm thresholds met; green-light decision |

## Teaching notes
- **Every scrimmage produces a scorecard and an AAR.** No debrief, no learning.
- The AAR format: what was scored, what the red team achieved, top 3 things to fix, who owns each fix
  and by when. Keep it blameless — focus on the system, not the person.
- Ramp red-team intensity deliberately: moderate → heavy → qualifier-like. Don't peak the difficulty
  before the team can handle it.
- By Week 23 confirm **two people can run every box.**
