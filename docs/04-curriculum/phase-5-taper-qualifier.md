# Phase 5 — Taper & Qualifier (Weeks 26–27, late Jan–Feb) + Regional Bridge

**Goal:** peak, don't cram. Lock logistics, keep skills warm with light drills, rest, and execute.

**Council lens:** the Coach and Former Tech Lead own the taper. The mistake teams make is exhausting
themselves the final week. Sharpness beats volume now.

**Exit gate:** compete at the **Minnesota CCDC state qualifier.**

## The taper (final ~2 weeks)

- **No new content. No exhausting scrimmages the final week.** Light skill-maintenance drills only —
  keep the credential sweep, persistence sweep, and first-30-minutes runbook warm.
- **Lock the roster:** starting 8 + alternates, roles confirmed, backups confirmed.
- **Logistics:** travel, setup time, equipment, accounts, what's allowed in-room (*confirm the rules
  packet*).
- **Prepare the in-competition kit** (print/stage): quick-reference, first-30-minutes runbook, inject
  templates, change-log, password tracker, contact list, scored-service list.
- **Pre-competition brief:** rules review, comms plan, "if X breaks, who owns it," and the injects
  ownership.
- **Rest.** A rested team makes fewer self-inflicted mistakes than a fried one.

## Regional Bridge (post-qualifier, if we advance to Midwest)

A short, intense block between state and regional. Regionals bring a **harder red team, more/faster
injects, and a larger service surface.**

- Target the specific weaknesses the qualifier exposed (drive it from the qualifier AAR).
- Deepen bench cross-training — regionals are longer and depth matters more.
- Raise scrimmage intensity above qualifier level.
- Same weekly rhythm, higher difficulty. Re-taper before regionals.

**Exit gate:** compete at the **Midwest Regional CCDC.**
